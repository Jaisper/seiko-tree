The Complete Seiko Mechanical Family Tree

This diagram maps Seiko's mechanical watch families using a curatorial three-era framework based on major company and technology milestones. Vintage Factory Era (1960–1988) covers the Suwa Seikosha and Daini/Kameido production systems — two rival in-house factories whose internal competition drove Grand Seiko, King Seiko, divers, and chronographs to world-class standards. Legacy Transition (1988–2001) traces the bridge period: Grand Seiko's dormancy and late-1990s mechanical revival, the 1999 commercial launch of Spring Drive, and the late legacy divers (7002, SKX) that kept mechanical Seiko alive through the quartz era. Modern Collection Era (2001–2025) begins with the establishment of Seiko Watch Corporation and shows today's brand and collection structure — Grand Seiko as an independent brand, the King Seiko revival, and the Prospex, Presage, and Seiko 5 Sports families. Era divisions are editorial and do not represent an official Seiko periodization. Edges show direct lineage , platform continuation , and heritage influence . Hover any edge for historical context.
